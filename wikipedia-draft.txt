{{Short description|Anonymous cinematic video creators}}

'''Kilmaru''' is the pseudonym used by an anonymous [[content creator]] — reported by several outlets to be a duo — known for first-person [[short-form video]]s that depict well-known cities and locations as if they were empty of all other people. The account, which also uses the handle ''kilianandmaruta'', has been active since 2021; the videos contain no spoken dialogue and no visible performer.<ref name="yahoo"/><ref name="geopop"/>

== Background ==
The "empty world" videos began appearing on [[TikTok]] in 2021.<ref name="yahoo"/> They are presented as the account of a narrator who describes himself as a former [[artificial intelligence]] programmer who, after hacking a quantum computer, is transported to an uninhabited parallel world.<ref name="geopop"/> In June 2021 a series in which the narrator claims to be living aboard a deserted cruise ship — described as a "ghost ship" — was reported by ''[[Newsweek]]'' and other outlets.<ref name="newsweek"/><ref name="yahoo"/> At the time of that coverage the account had about 2.3 million TikTok followers;<ref name="yahoo"/> a 2024 Italian report put the following at nearly four million on TikTok and around 680,000 on [[Instagram]].<ref name="geopop"/>

== Content and style ==
Filmed in the first person, the videos show deserted versions of locations such as the [[Colosseum]], New York and London, along with airports, hotels, stadiums and cruise ships.<ref name="geopop"/><ref name="newsweek"/> The performer is never shown and there is no dialogue. The empty-location effect has been attributed to filming at quiet times and to removing remaining people in [[post-production]] using software tools; the fact-checking website ''[[Snopes]]'' reached a similar conclusion about the videos.<ref name="geopop"/><ref name="snopes"/>

== Reception ==
The videos received coverage in several countries. In Italy they were reported by ''[[Corriere della Sera]]'', ''[[Wired (magazine)|Wired]]'', ''[[Fanpage.it|Fanpage]]'', ''Geopop'' and ''Lo Speciale''.<ref name="corriere"/><ref name="wired"/><ref name="fanpage"/><ref name="geopop"/><ref name="lospeciale"/> In the United Kingdom they were covered by ''[[The Sun (United Kingdom)|The Sun]]'',<ref name="thesun"/> in the United States by ''Newsweek'' and Yahoo's ''In the Know'',<ref name="newsweek"/><ref name="yahoo"/> and in France by ''[[20 Minutes (France)|20 Minutes]]''.<ref name="20min"/> The visual-effects channel [[Corridor Crew]] and the online-mystery analysis channel Night Mind each produced videos about the work.<ref name="corridor"/><ref name="nightmind"/>

== Awards ==
At the 2021 [[Monaco]] Streaming Film Festival, the work received the award for Best Creative Content, credited to the duo "Kilian and Maruta".<ref name="monaco"/>
